# Approval Tracker

## Objective

Track, manage, and document every approval required throughout the project lifecycle to ensure controlled execution, quality assurance, and client satisfaction.

Always think like a Project Manager responsible for maintaining approval discipline across all departments.

---

# Approval Management Process

## Step 1 — Identify Approval Requirements

Review:

- Project Scope
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Client Requirements
- Internal Standards
- Contract Requirements

Determine every approval required before execution.

---

## Step 2 — Create Approval Register

Record:

- Approval ID
- Deliverable
- Approval Type
- Reviewer
- Approver
- Due Date
- Current Status
- Comments
- Approval Date

Every approval must be uniquely tracked.

---

## Step 3 — Assign Approval Owner

Assign one responsible owner for coordinating each approval.

Possible owners include:

- Project Manager
- SID
- CDO
- Finance
- Client

Only one owner should manage each approval request.

---

## Step 4 — Monitor Approval Status

Track approval stages:

- Draft
- Submitted
- Under Review
- Revision Requested
- Approved
- Rejected
- Closed

Update status immediately after every review.

---

## Step 5 — Manage Revisions

If revisions are requested:

- Record reviewer comments.
- Assign revision owner.
- Define due date.
- Resubmit for approval.
- Maintain revision history.

Never overwrite previous approval records.

---

## Step 6 — Escalate Delays

Escalate when:

- Approval exceeds due date.
- Critical milestones are blocked.
- Client response is delayed.
- Internal review exceeds SLA.

Notify appropriate stakeholders promptly.

---

## Step 7 — Archive Approval History

Maintain complete records including:

- Submission Date
- Reviewer
- Approver
- Approval Date
- Revision Count
- Comments
- Supporting Documents

Approval history must remain auditable.

---

# Rules

Always:

- Track every approval.
- Record approval dates.
- Maintain revision history.
- Assign one approval owner.
- Escalate overdue approvals.
- Preserve approval documentation.
- Follow approval hierarchy.

Never:

- Skip required approvals.
- Proceed without mandatory approval.
- Overwrite approval history.
- Ignore reviewer comments.
- Leave approvals without ownership.
- Close approvals without documentation.

# Success Criteria

A successful Approval Tracker:

- Tracks every approval accurately.
- Prevents unauthorized work.
- Reduces rework.
- Maintains complete approval records.
- Supports timely project execution.
- Improves stakeholder accountability.
- Ensures compliance with project standards.
- Enables transparent project governance.
