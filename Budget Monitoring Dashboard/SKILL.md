# Budget Monitoring Dashboard

## Objective

Monitor, compare, analyze, and forecast financial budgets across projects, departments, and the organization to ensure spending remains within approved limits while protecting profitability.

Always think like a Financial Controller responsible for ensuring every project stays financially healthy.

---

# Budget Monitoring Process

## Step 1 — Establish Budget Baseline

Collect and verify:

- Approved Project Budget
- Department Budget
- Company Operating Budget
- Client Approved Value
- Cost Estimate
- BOQ Value
- Contingency Allocation

The approved budget becomes the financial baseline.

---

## Step 2 — Track Budget Allocation

Allocate budgets into categories such as:

- Interior Design
- Civil Work
- Modular Furniture
- Electrical
- Plumbing
- False Ceiling
- Painting
- Flooring
- Lighting
- Soft Furnishing
- Labour
- Site Management
- Transportation
- Marketing
- Administration
- Office Operations

Every budget allocation must be traceable.

---

## Step 3 — Compare Budget vs Actual

Calculate:

- Planned Budget
- Actual Spending
- Remaining Budget
- Budget Utilization (%)
- Cost Variance
- Revenue Variance

Highlight significant deviations immediately.

---

## Step 4 — Monitor Budget Performance

Evaluate:

- Project Budget Health
- Department Spending
- Monthly Spending Trend
- Budget Burn Rate
- Cost Efficiency
- Resource Utilization

Identify early warning signs before overruns occur.

---

## Step 5 — Forecast Remaining Budget

Estimate:

- Remaining Project Cost
- Expected Future Expenses
- Cash Requirements
- Revised Completion Cost
- Forecasted Budget Position

Forecasts should be updated whenever significant changes occur.

---

## Step 6 — Variance Analysis

Analyze reasons for budget differences.

Examples:

- Scope Change
- Client Variations
- Material Price Increase
- Labour Cost Increase
- Design Revision
- Vendor Delay
- Procurement Changes
- Estimation Error

Document corrective actions.

---

## Step 7 — Budget Alerts

Trigger alerts when:

- Budget exceeds 80%
- Budget exceeds 90%
- Budget exceeded
- Contingency exhausted
- Revenue below forecast
- Expenses unusually high

Alerts should support proactive decision-making.

---

## Step 8 — Management Dashboard

Prepare dashboards for:

- CEO
- COO
- Finance Manager
- Project Manager

Display:

- Budget Status
- Project Health
- Cost Variance
- Remaining Budget
- Forecast Cost
- Profitability Indicator

Use concise executive-level reporting.

---

## Step 9 — Recommendations

Provide actionable recommendations such as:

- Reduce discretionary spending.
- Review procurement strategy.
- Reallocate unused budget.
- Negotiate vendor pricing.
- Optimize labour allocation.
- Request client approval for variation work.
- Revise project forecast.

Recommendations should be practical and financially justified.

# Rules

Always:

- Use approved budgets as the baseline.
- Compare actual spending with planned budgets.
- Investigate significant variances.
- Update forecasts regularly.
- Maintain historical budget revisions.
- Report cost overruns immediately.
- Protect project profitability.
- Provide management recommendations.

Never:

- Modify approved budgets without authorization.
- Ignore budget overruns.
- Exclude committed costs.
- Forecast without current financial data.
- Hide negative variances.
- Delete historical budget records.

- Tracks every approved budget accurately.
- Detects cost overruns early.
- Provides real-time financial visibility.
- Supports proactive management decisions.
- Improves budget discipline.
- Protects project profitability.
- Enhances forecasting accuracy.
- Meets Vayra Atelier's financial governance standards.
