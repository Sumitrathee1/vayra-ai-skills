# Meeting MOM Generator

## Objective

Generate clear, structured, and actionable Minutes of Meeting (MOM) that accurately document discussions, decisions, responsibilities, deadlines, and follow-up actions.

Always think like a professional Project Manager responsible for maintaining complete meeting records and ensuring accountability.

---

# MOM Generation Process

## Step 1 — Capture Meeting Details

Record:

- Meeting Title
- Meeting Type
- Date
- Time
- Location / Platform
- Organizer
- Participants
- Absentees (if any)

Every meeting must have complete identification details.

---

## Step 2 — Define Meeting Objective

Clearly document:

- Purpose of the meeting
- Expected outcomes
- Key topics to be discussed

The objective should align with the project stage.

---

## Step 3 — Record Agenda

List agenda items in logical order.

Examples:

- Project Progress Review
- Design Discussion
- Layout Approval
- Material Selection
- Budget Review
- Site Progress
- Pending Issues
- Client Feedback
- Next Milestones

---

## Step 4 — Document Discussion Points

Summarize important discussions.

For each discussion include:

- Topic
- Key observations
- Alternatives considered
- Concerns raised
- Recommendations

Keep discussions factual and concise.

---

## Step 5 — Record Decisions

Document every confirmed decision.

Each decision should include:

- Decision ID
- Description
- Approved By
- Approval Date
- Effective From
- Impacted Deliverables

Only finalized decisions should appear in this section.

---

## Step 6 — Create Action Items

Every action item should include:

- Action ID
- Task Description
- Responsible Person
- Due Date
- Priority
- Dependencies
- Current Status

Ensure action items are measurable and achievable.

---

## Step 7 — Record Risks & Issues

Document:

- Newly Identified Risks
- Active Issues
- Escalations
- Blockers
- Mitigation Actions

Reference existing Risk Register and Issue Tracker where applicable.

---

## Step 8 — Define Follow-up

Specify:

- Pending Items
- Required Documents
- Upcoming Deliverables
- Next Review Date
- Next Meeting Schedule

Follow-up items should be assigned to specific owners.

---

## Step 9 — Generate Meeting Summary

Summarize:

- Major Decisions
- Completed Actions
- Outstanding Items
- Key Risks
- Overall Meeting Outcome

Keep the summary brief and executive-friendly.

---

# Standard MOM Structure

1. Meeting Information
2. Participants
3. Meeting Objective
4. Agenda
5. Discussion Summary
6. Decisions Made
7. Action Items
8. Risks & Issues
9. Follow-up Actions
10. Next Meeting Details
11. Meeting Summary

---

# Rules

Always:

- Record facts objectively.
- Document confirmed decisions only.
- Assign action owners.
- Include due dates.
- Maintain chronological order.
- Keep language professional.
- Highlight pending actions.
- Reference related project documents where required.

Never:

- Record assumptions as decisions.
- Leave action items without owners.
- Omit deadlines.
- Use informal language.
- Modify approved decisions.
- Exclude unresolved issues.

---

# Deliverables

Generate:

- Minutes of Meeting (MOM)
- Decision Log
- Action Item Register
- Follow-up Tracker
- Attendance Register
- Discussion Summary
- Meeting Summary

---

# Success Criteria

A successful MOM:

- Accurately captures meeting discussions.
- Documents all approved decisions.
- Assigns clear responsibilities.
- Defines realistic deadlines.
- Tracks follow-up actions.
- Improves team accountability.
- Reduces communication gaps.
- Serves as an official project record.
