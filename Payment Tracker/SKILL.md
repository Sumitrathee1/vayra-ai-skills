# Payment Tracker

## Objective

Track every client payment from invoice generation to final settlement while maintaining accurate financial records, supporting cash flow management, and ensuring timely payment collections.

Always think like a Finance Controller responsible for maintaining healthy receivables and minimizing overdue payments.

---

# Payment Tracking Process

## Step 1 — Verify Invoice

Review:

- Invoice Number
- Client Name
- Project Name
- Invoice Date
- Due Date
- Invoice Amount
- GST Amount
- Total Payable

Ensure the invoice has been officially approved and issued.

---

## Step 2 — Record Payment

Capture:

- Payment Date
- Amount Received
- Payment Method
- Bank Reference Number
- Transaction ID
- UTR Number (if applicable)
- Received By

Every payment must be recorded immediately after confirmation.

---

## Step 3 — Update Client Ledger

Update:

- Total Invoice Value
- Total Payments Received
- Outstanding Balance
- Advance Balance (if any)
- Credit Notes
- Refunds (if applicable)

Maintain an accurate financial history for every client.

---

## Step 4 — Monitor Due Dates

Track:

- Upcoming Due Payments
- Due Today
- Overdue Payments
- Days Outstanding
- Collection Status

Identify payment delays before they become critical.

---

## Step 5 — Payment Follow-up

Categorize collection status:

- Payment Scheduled
- Reminder Sent
- Client Follow-up
- Escalated
- Payment Received
- Closed

Record every collection activity.

---

## Step 6 — Reconcile Payments

Verify:

- Bank Statement
- Invoice Amount
- GST Amount
- Outstanding Balance
- Duplicate Payments
- Partial Payments

Ensure all payments are accurately reconciled.

---

## Step 7 — Generate Reports

Prepare:

- Outstanding Report
- Accounts Receivable Report
- Client Payment History
- Overdue Summary
- Monthly Collection Report
- Cash Collection Summary

Reports should be available on demand.

---

## Step 8 — Escalation Management

Escalate payments based on company policy.

Suggested workflow:

- 3 Days Before Due → Friendly Reminder
- Due Date → Payment Reminder
- 7 Days Overdue → Follow-up Call
- 15 Days Overdue → Management Escalation
- 30+ Days Overdue → Commercial Action

Follow company-approved collection procedures.

# Rules

Always:

- Record payments immediately.
- Verify bank transactions.
- Track outstanding balances.
- Maintain complete payment history.
- Follow approved reminder schedules.
- Reconcile invoices with payments.
- Update financial dashboards.

Never:

- Delete payment records.
- Modify historical transactions.
- Ignore overdue invoices.
- Mark invoices as paid without confirmation.
- Leave client ledgers outdated.
- Skip reconciliation.

# Success Criteria

A successful payment tracking system:

- Tracks every invoice accurately.
- Maintains real-time outstanding balances.
- Reduces overdue payments.
- Improves collection efficiency.
- Supports healthy cash flow.
- Provides complete financial transparency.
- Enables accurate financial reporting.
- Meets Vayra Atelier's financial control standards.
