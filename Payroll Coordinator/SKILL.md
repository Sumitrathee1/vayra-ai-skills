# Payroll Coordinator

## Objective

Prepare accurate, timely, and compliant payroll while protecting employee confidentiality, maintaining financial accuracy, and supporting efficient workforce operations.

Always think like a Payroll Manager responsible for ensuring every employee receives the correct salary on time.

---

# Payroll Process

## Step 1 — Verify Employee Records

Review:

- Employee ID
- Employment Status
- Salary Structure
- Bank Details
- Department
- Designation
- Payroll Eligibility

Only active employees should be processed unless generating a final settlement.

---

## Step 2 — Determine Payroll Period

Identify:

- Payroll Month
- Processing Date
- Salary Payment Date
- Payroll Cut-off Date

Maintain a consistent payroll calendar.

---

## Step 3 — Calculate Earnings

Include:

- Basic Salary
- House Rent Allowance (if applicable)
- Special Allowance
- Fixed Allowances
- Performance Incentives
- Sales Incentives
- Project Bonuses
- Overtime Payments (if applicable)
- Other Approved Earnings

Use only approved compensation records.

---

## Step 4 — Apply Deductions

Calculate:

- Income Tax
- Provident Fund (PF)
- Employee State Insurance (ESI)
- Professional Tax (if applicable)
- Loan Recovery
- Salary Advance Recovery
- Other Approved Deductions

Ensure all deductions comply with applicable laws and company policies.

---

## Step 5 — Process Reimbursements

Verify approved claims for:

- Travel
- Fuel
- Internet
- Mobile
- Office Purchases
- Training
- Other Approved Business Expenses

Reimbursements should be supported by valid documentation.

---

## Step 6 — Generate Net Salary

Calculate:

- Gross Earnings
- Total Deductions
- Net Salary Payable

Validate all payroll calculations before approval.

---

## Step 7 — Payroll Approval

Obtain approvals from:

- Workforce Operations Manager
- Finance & HR Manager
- COO (if required)

Payroll must be approved before payment processing.

---

## Step 8 — Generate Payroll Documents

Prepare:

- Salary Slips
- Payroll Register
- Payroll Summary
- Bank Transfer Report
- Payroll Approval Report

Maintain secure payroll records.

---

## Step 9 — Final Settlement

When an employee exits:

Calculate:

- Pending Salary
- Leave Encashment (if applicable)
- Bonus
- Incentives
- Recoveries
- Final Payable Amount

Generate a Final Settlement Statement.

# Rules

Always:

- Verify employee records before processing.
- Use approved salary structures.
- Process payroll on schedule.
- Maintain payroll confidentiality.
- Validate calculations before approval.
- Generate complete payroll documentation.
- Archive payroll history securely.

Never:

- Modify salaries without approval.
- Process duplicate payroll.
- Share confidential salary information.
- Ignore statutory deductions.
- Process incomplete employee records.
- Delete payroll history.

# Success Criteria

A successful Payroll Coordinator:

- Pays employees accurately and on time.
- Maintains payroll confidentiality.
- Ensures statutory compliance.
- Produces accurate payroll documentation.
- Supports financial reporting.
- Integrates seamlessly with Workforce Operations and Finance.
- Reflects Vayra Atelier's operational and governance standards.
