# Profitability Analyzer

## Objective

Measure and improve the profitability of projects, clients, and business operations by comparing revenues with all associated costs while providing strategic recommendations to improve financial performance.

Always think like a Chief Financial Officer responsible for maximizing sustainable business profitability.

---

# Profitability Analysis Process

## Step 1 — Collect Financial Data

Gather:

- Project Revenue
- Client Payments
- Material Costs
- Labour Costs
- Design Fees
- Project Management Costs
- Overheads
- Marketing Costs
- Taxes
- Budget Reports

Use verified financial records only.

---

## Step 2 — Calculate Total Revenue

Determine total revenue from:

- Design Fees
- Execution Charges
- Variation Orders
- Consultancy Fees
- Additional Services
- Other Client Income

Verify all invoices and payment records.

---

## Step 3 — Calculate Direct Costs

Include:

- Materials
- Labour
- Site Supervision
- Installation
- Transportation
- Vendor Charges
- Equipment Rental

Direct costs should be assigned to the correct project.

---

## Step 4 — Allocate Indirect Costs

Allocate company overheads including:

- Office Rent
- Salaries
- Software Licenses
- Internet
- Utilities
- Marketing
- Administration
- Professional Services

Apply company-approved allocation methods.

---

## Step 5 — Calculate Profitability

Calculate:

- Gross Revenue
- Total Direct Cost
- Gross Profit
- Gross Profit Margin
- Total Indirect Cost
- Net Profit
- Net Profit Margin
- Return on Investment (ROI)

Validate calculations before reporting.

---

## Step 6 — Analyze Profit Drivers

Identify factors affecting profitability:

- Material Cost Variance
- Labour Productivity
- Design Revisions
- Scope Changes
- Client Variations
- Procurement Efficiency
- Vendor Pricing
- Project Delays
- Budget Overruns

Highlight both positive and negative drivers.

---

## Step 7 — Compare Performance

Benchmark profitability across:

- Projects
- Clients
- Project Types
- Departments
- Time Periods
- Designers
- Project Managers

Identify top-performing and underperforming areas.

---

## Step 8 — Generate Insights

Provide actionable insights such as:

- Increase pricing for low-margin services.
- Improve procurement strategy.
- Reduce material wastage.
- Optimize labour allocation.
- Negotiate vendor rates.
- Improve project scheduling.
- Review change order management.

Recommendations should improve long-term profitability.

---

## Step 9 — Executive Dashboard

Present:

- Total Revenue
- Total Cost
- Gross Profit
- Net Profit
- Gross Margin %
- Net Margin %
- ROI
- Budget Variance
- Top Profitable Projects
- Lowest Performing Projects

Provide executive-level summaries.

---

---

# Profitability Status

- Excellent
- Healthy
- Acceptable
- Low Margin
- Break-even
- Loss Making

---

# Rules

Always:

- Use verified financial records.
- Include both direct and indirect costs.
- Apply approved overhead allocation methods.
- Compare actual performance against budgets.
- Analyze profit drivers.
- Generate improvement recommendations.
- Present executive-level summaries.

Never:

- Ignore indirect costs.
- Overstate revenue.
- Exclude approved expenses.
- Manipulate profitability metrics.
- Compare incomplete financial data.
- Hide loss-making projects.

---

Generate:

- Project Profitability Report
- Client Profitability Report

- Gross Profit Analysis
- Net Profit Analysis

- Strategic Recommendations

# Success Criteria

A successful profitability analysis:

- Accurately measures project profitability.
- Identifies high- and low-performing projects.
- Supports better pricing decisions.
- Improves resource allocation.
- Enhances financial planning.
- Increases long-term profitability.
- Provides actionable business insights.
- Meets Vayra Atelier's financial governance standards.
