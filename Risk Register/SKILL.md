# Risk Register

## Objective

Identify, assess, prioritize, and manage project risks before they negatively impact cost, quality, timeline, or client satisfaction.

Always think like an experienced Project Manager who anticipates problems before they occur.

---

# Risk Management Process

## Step 1 — Understand the Project

Review:

- Project Scope
- Timeline
- Budget
- Team Structure
- Site Conditions
- Client Expectations
- Procurement Plan

Understand project constraints before identifying risks.

---

## Step 2 — Identify Risks

Identify possible risks across all project areas.

Examples:

### Project Risks

- Scope creep
- Unrealistic deadlines
- Resource shortages
- Budget overruns

### Design Risks

- Frequent revisions
- Approval delays
- Incomplete drawings
- Material incompatibility

### Site Risks

- Measurement errors
- Structural issues
- Utility conflicts
- Site access restrictions

### Procurement Risks

- Material shortages
- Vendor delays
- Price fluctuations
- Damaged deliveries

### Client Risks

- Delayed approvals
- Payment delays
- Requirement changes
- Communication gaps

### Team Risks

- Resource unavailability
- Skill gaps
- Workload imbalance
- Miscommunication

---

## Step 3 — Assess Risks

Evaluate each risk based on:

- Probability
- Impact
- Urgency
- Detectability

Classify risk levels as:

- Critical
- High
- Medium
- Low

---

## Step 4 — Assign Risk Owner

Every risk must have one owner responsible for:

- Monitoring
- Mitigation
- Escalation
- Reporting
- Closure

---

## Step 5 — Define Mitigation Plan

For each risk, specify:

- Preventive actions
- Corrective actions
- Responsible person
- Timeline
- Required resources

Mitigation should reduce either the likelihood or the impact of the risk.

---

## Step 6 — Define Contingency Plan

Prepare backup actions if the risk occurs.

Examples:

- Alternate vendor
- Buffer timeline
- Additional workforce
- Design alternative
- Temporary workaround

---

## Step 7 — Monitor Risks

Review risks:

- Weekly
- At milestone completion
- After scope changes
- After client meetings
- During site inspections

Update risk status regularly.

---

## Step 8 — Close Risks

A risk may be closed when:

- It no longer exists.
- Mitigation is successful.
- The project phase is complete.
- The contingency plan has resolved the issue.

Document lessons learned before closure.

---

# Risk Categories

Always classify risks into:

- Scope
- Schedule
- Budget
- Design
- Technical
- Procurement
- Site Execution
- Client
- Vendor
- Resource
- Quality
- Safety
- Legal
- Environmental

---

# Rules

Always:

- Identify risks early.
- Prioritize objectively.
- Assign one risk owner.
- Create mitigation plans.
- Define contingency actions.
- Review risks regularly.
- Escalate critical risks immediately.

Never:

- Ignore minor risks.
- Leave risks without ownership.
- Delay mitigation planning.
- Underestimate high-impact risks.
- Close risks without verification.

---

# Deliverables

Generate:

- Risk Register
- Risk Matrix
- Priority Assessment
- Mitigation Plan
- Contingency Plan
- Risk Owner List
- Risk Review Schedule

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Risk Register:

- Identifies project threats early.
- Prioritizes risks accurately.
- Assigns ownership.
- Reduces project uncertainty.
- Improves decision-making.
- Protects project quality.
- Minimizes schedule delays.
- Supports successful project delivery.
