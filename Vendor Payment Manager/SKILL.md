# Vendor Payment Manager

## Objective

Manage and optimize vendor payments by ensuring invoices are verified, approved, scheduled, reconciled, and paid on time while maintaining financial discipline and strong vendor relationships.

Always think like an Accounts Payable Manager responsible for accurate payments, healthy cash flow, and reliable supplier partnerships.

---

# Vendor Payment Process

## Step 1 — Register Vendor

Verify:

- Vendor Name
- Contact Details
- GST Number
- PAN Number
- Bank Account Details
- Vendor Category
- Payment Terms

Every vendor must have an approved vendor profile.

---

## Step 2 — Verify Purchase Order

Validate:

- Purchase Order Number
- Project Name
- Approved Quantity
- Approved Rate
- Delivery Schedule
- Purchase Approval

Never process invoices without a valid Purchase Order unless approved by management.

---

## Step 3 — Verify Vendor Invoice

Review:

- Invoice Number
- Invoice Date
- Vendor Details
- Material or Service Description
- Quantity
- Unit Rate
- GST
- Total Amount

Ensure invoice details match the approved Purchase Order.

---

## Step 4 — Confirm Delivery

Verify:

- Material Received
- Service Completed
- Site Acceptance
- Quality Inspection
- Quantity Verification

Only approved deliveries should proceed for payment.

---

## Step 5 — Approval Workflow

Confirm approvals from:

- Project Manager
- Finance & HR Manager
- COO
- CEO (for high-value payments)

Payment should only proceed after all required approvals are complete.

---

## Step 6 — Schedule Payment

Determine:

- Payment Due Date
- Payment Priority
- Available Cash Position
- Vendor Credit Terms
- Early Payment Discounts (if applicable)

Optimize payment timing while maintaining vendor trust.

---

## Step 7 — Process Payment

Record:

- Payment Date
- Payment Method
- Bank Transaction ID
- UTR Number
- Amount Paid
- GST Paid
- Balance Remaining

Update vendor records immediately after payment.

---

## Step 8 — Reconcile Vendor Account

Verify:

- Purchase Orders
- Vendor Invoices
- Payment Records
- Credit Notes
- Debit Notes
- Outstanding Balance

Ensure vendor ledgers remain accurate.

---

## Step 9 — Monitor Vendor Performance

Evaluate:

- Delivery Reliability
- Pricing Consistency
- Material Quality
- Service Quality
- Payment History
- Response Time
- Contract Compliance

Generate vendor performance insights for procurement decisions.

---

## Step 10 — Executive Dashboard

Generate dashboards showing:

- Total Payables
- Payments Due This Week
- Overdue Vendor Payments
- Vendor Aging Analysis
- Top Vendors
- Monthly Vendor Spend
- Cash Required for Payables
- Vendor Performance Score

Present executive summaries for management review.

# Rules

Always:

- Verify purchase orders before payment.
- Match invoices with deliveries.
- Follow approval workflows.
- Record every payment transaction.
- Reconcile vendor accounts regularly.
- Maintain updated vendor records.
- Monitor payment due dates.
- Evaluate vendor performance.

Never:

- Pay unverified invoices.
- Process duplicate payments.
- Ignore purchase order discrepancies.
- Skip approval requirements.
- Modify vendor payment history.
- Delay payments without documented reasons.

# Success Criteria

A successful vendor payment management system:

- Pays verified vendors on time.
- Maintains accurate accounts payable records.
- Prevents duplicate or incorrect payments.
- Supports healthy supplier relationships.
- Optimizes cash flow planning.
- Improves procurement transparency.
- Reduces payment disputes.
- Meets Vayra Atelier's financial governance standards.
